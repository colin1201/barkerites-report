Drop the game file from your sports watch and get your personal match report — heat map, quarter-by-quarter movement, sprints, and heart-rate zones.
Tap to choose your game file
or drag it here · accepts .gpx and .tcx
🔒 Processed on your device — your file is never uploaded or stored anywhere.

How to get your file (~1 minute)

Garmin: open connect.garmin.com in your phone's browser (the export lives on the website, not the app) → log in → open the game activity → tap the gear icon → Export to GPX. The file downloads — come back here and drop it in.
Strava: open the activity on strava.com → ••• menu → Export GPX.
No file? You need to record the game on a GPS sports watch (Garmin, Coros, Suunto, Apple Watch via a GPX export app).
Just set "Match started" to the real push-back time (e.g. 19:35) — halftime and match end fill in automatically. Only touch the other two if the game ran long or the map looks smeared across both flanks.

Breaks & bench (auto-detected)

Stretches where you moved at walking pace or slower. Halftime and full sit-downs are pre-ticked; tick any window where you were actually off the pitch — subbed to the bench, a card sit-out, a long break. Leave a window unticked if you were playing (quiet positioning counts as play). Ticked windows are left out of the heat maps and stats.
